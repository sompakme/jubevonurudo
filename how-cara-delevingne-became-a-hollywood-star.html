If you think that success comes with a price, Hollywood star Cara Delevingne would beg to differ. From modeling to acting, this British beauty with lush beginnings has mastered the art of success and is now worth a whopping $50 million! While many may assume her fortune is merely the result of her striking looks and effortless talent, there's more to this story than meets the eye. In this article, we'll explore the ins and outs of how Cara Delevingne made her fortune and uncover the secrets to her success. So, buckle up, and let's dive into the glamorous world of this starlet-turned-mogul!

A Look at Cara Delevingne’s Early Days

Growing up in the posh Belgravia area of London, Cara was born into a family of high achievers. Her father, Charles Hamar Delevingne, is a property developer, while her maternal grandfather, Sir Jocelyn Stevens, was a publishing executive and chairman of English Heritage. It's safe to say that success was in her blood. However, Cara's path to success wasn't always smooth sailing. She attended Francis Holland School for Girls until the age of 16 before moving to Bedales School in Hampshire to focus on drama and music.

However, schooling wasn't easy for Cara, who has dyspraxia and found it challenging. In fact, she battled depression and anxiety when she was just 15 years old, and at one point, even resorted to slamming her head against a tree to cope with the pain. Good thing Cara didn't let her struggles define her. Instead, she delved into modeling, following in the footsteps of her older sister, Poppy, and the rest, as they say, is history. Now, how did Cara make her $55 million fortune? Let’s find out!

Cara’s Rewarding Modeling Career

From a Vogue Italia editorial at age ten to strutting her stuff on the world's most iconic runways, Cara Delevingne has carved her way to the top of the modeling world. After signing with Storm Management in 2009, she worked for a year before landing her first paying gig; patience is indeed a virtue. In 2012, her career skyrocketed when Burberry's Christopher Bailey discovered her while she was working part-time in the office of a fashion website. From there, she became a regular face on the runway circuit, stealing the show at major fashion weeks in New York, London, Milan, and Paris.

The fashion elite couldn't get enough of her, and she found herself walking for the biggest names in the game, including Fendi, DSquared2, Dolce & Gabbana, and Chanel. That's not all, she even strutted her stuff in the Victoria's Secret Fashion Show, earning a pretty penny for each appearance. Her relationship with Karl Lagerfeld blossomed, and she walked in Chanel's Haute-Couture spring show, stole the show at the Valentino Haute Couture show, and even landed appearances at Louis Vuitton and Hakaan during Paris Fashion Week. Cara's modeling career has undoubtedly paid off and her net worth is a testament to that. This talented powerhouse has proven that dreams can come true with hard work and dedication.

Cara Delevingne’s Success on the Big Screens

Cara Delevingne has taken the film industry by storm with her remarkable acting skills. Delevingne's career began in 2012 when she played Princess Sorokina in the film adaptation of Anna Karenina. She then went on to play the role of Melanie in the British psychological thriller The Face of an Angel, which many critics praised for her sweet and playful on-screen presence. Delevingne also made her TV debut in the final episode of Playhouse Presents and in 2015, she co-starred as Margo Roth Spiegelman in the film adaptation of John Green's novel, Paper Towns, where her acting skills were lauded as a real find of the film.

Her versatility as an actress was then showcased in her roles as a mermaid in Pan, a villainess in Suicide Squad, and a lead role in Valerian and the City of a Thousand Planets. Delevingne's recent work includes the female lead role in the Amazon Prime Video fantasy steampunk series Carnival Row, where she plays the faerie war refugee, Vignette Stonemoss. It's clear that Delevingne's acting prowess has established her as a formidable presence on the big screen with many intriguing roles to her credit. Even her impressive $50 million fortune is a testament to that.

Cara’s Success with Appealing Commercials

Cara's success isn't limited to just her talents in modeling and acting, she's also a star in the world of commercials. From promoting cosmetics for Sephora in the United States to showcasing the latest trends in Rimmel cosmetics in the United Kingdom, Delevingne has conquered the world of beauty. That's not all, she has also starred in commercials for video games, ice cream, handbags, shoes, and even earphones. Her charisma and style are put to use in promoting products such as Call of Duty: Black Ops III for Activision, Magnum Double ice cream, Gabrielle handbags for Chanel, and Beats by Dre earphones for Apple. With her unique blend of beauty, talent, and charm, Cara has become a go-to star for companies looking to create commercials that truly stand out.

Writing, Music, Fashion, and Other Ventures

Cara Delevingne is a true jack-of-all-trades. This multi-talented starlet has dabbled in modeling, acting, writing, music, and fashion, proving that she's not just a pretty face - she's a money-making machine! She's a published author, co-writing a young adult novel with Rowan Coleman that tackles the ups and downs of teenage life. While she has earned a lot of money from modeling, acting, and writing, Cara can't just stop being creative.

Cara has recorded two music albums and even turned down a record deal because they wanted to change her name. She's also lent her voice to some big-name artists, like St. Vincent and Fiona Apple. That's not all, Delevingne's also got some fashion cred as she has designed collections for DKNY and Mulberry, and even teamed up with Pharrell Williams for a Chanel short film. It is also worth mentioning that she has also launched her own line of vegan Prosecco which is earning her a cool amount of money. Talk about a Renaissance woman!
