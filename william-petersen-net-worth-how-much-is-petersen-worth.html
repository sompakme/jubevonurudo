Early Life

William Petersen, born on February 21, 1953, in Evanston, Illinois, had an eventful early life that shaped his journey towards becoming a successful actor. Growing up in a Roman Catholic household, Petersen attended Bishop Kelly High School in Idaho. As a talented athlete, he earned a football scholarship to Idaho State University. However, his passion for the arts led him to choose a different path.

Determined to pursue his dreams, Petersen embarked on a transformative journey in Spain’s Basque country, where he studied Shakespearean acting. This experience broadened his horizons and honed his skills, preparing him for what was to come in his career.

Upon returning to the United States, Petersen found his artistic calling in the vibrant theater scene of Chicago. He became involved with the renowned Steppenwolf Theatre Company, an influential ensemble known for its groundbreaking performances.
