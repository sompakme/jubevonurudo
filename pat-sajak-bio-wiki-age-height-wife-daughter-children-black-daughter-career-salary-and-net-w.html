Pat Sajak Biography

Pat Sajak is an American Television Personality and Game Show Host, who was born and brought up in Chicago, Illinois, United States of America. He is widely known as the host of the American television game show ‘Wheel of Fortune’ since 1981. He broke the world record on May 8, 2019, for having the longest career as a game show host for the same show. By then he had hosted Wheel of Fortune for 38 years and 198 days.

    Pat Sajak Young and Career Inception

    Sajak won a contest that was going on, on WLS radio’s Dick Biondi Show to be a guest teen deejay. While schooling at Columbia College Chicago, Al Parker, who was Sajak’s broadcasting instructor told him that a local radio station (WEDC) was looking for a newsman.

    After applying for the job, he was hired to work from midnight to 6:00 AM. Later in 1968, he joined the US Army and was sent to Vietnam. While in Vietnam, Sajak served as a disc jockey on Armed Forces Radio.

    In the early 1970s, he DJed for a Murray, Kentucky, radio station for a year. Sajak also began DJ’ing at 50,000-watt WSM in Nashville. He was the 3:00–5:00 pm afternoon personality.

    The television sister of the radio station is the one that brought Sajak on-screen. At first, Sajak was a voiceover artist. He was doing station identifications and anchoring the five-minute newscasts during NBC’s Today Show.

    He later went on to be a weekend and substitute weatherman, where he became acquainted with anchor Dan Miller. In 1977, Sajak moved to KNBC-TV in Los Angeles to be a full-time weatherman for the station.

    Pat Sajak Vanna White Wheel Of Fortune

    Wheel of Fortune originally aired as a daytime series on NBC from January 6, 1975, to June 30, 1989. After some changes were made to its format, the daytime series from July 17, 1989, to January 11, 1991, moved to CBS.

    In 1981, Sajak was asked by Merv Griffin if he would be interested in taking over the duties as a host on Wheel of Fortune from Chuck Woolery.

    However, Fred Silverman, who was the president and CEO of NBC at the time, rejected Sajak’s hiring, claiming he was too local. Griffin responded by imposing a moratorium on new tapings until Sajak was hired.

    From 1983 to 1989, Sajak hosted both the daytime (NBC) and syndicated evening versions of Wheel of Fortune. He continues to host Wheel of Fortune to date.

    With him returning for his 36th season in 2018–19, Sajak became the longest-running host of any game show. He surpassed Bob Barker, who hosted Price Is Right from 1972 to 2007. He was officially honored as such by the Guinness World Records with the episode taped March 22, 2019.

    For the last 37 years, Vanna White has been back and forth across the “Wheel of Fortune” stage, showing tens of thousands of consonants and vowels and making five-inch heels look easy.

    Vanna White and Pat Sajak have been the hosts of the show (Wheel Of Fortune) since the 1980s. The show’s primary formation has not changed since Vanna White spun her first letter in 1982: Participants spin a large carnival-style wheel for a possibility to solve word puzzles for cash and prizes.

    In 1997, the board advanced to digital, and White’s job shifted from turning letters to just tapping them awake. The “Wheel of Fortune” has grown a little more complex over the years, with mini-games inside the game, including “crossword rounds” (intertwined words joined by a clue) and “triple toss-ups” (one round with three quick puzzles).

    Vanna White had to handle all of those on top of balancing the host’s core duties of maintaining the fast-paced game moving along and paying close observation in case contestants incorrectly made a word plural or added a word to a phrase. These were duties that White, who had only one dusty run before filming, “subconsciously understood but had to be very aware of.”
