Who is Mark Levin? Mark Levin Biography and Wiki

Mark Reed Levin professionally known as Mark Levin is an American lawyer, author, and radio personality who was born in Philadelphia, PA, United States of America. Currently, he works as a host of the syndicated radio show The Mark Levin Show, as well as Life, Liberty & Levin on Fox News. Previously, he worked in the administration of President Ronald Reagan and was a chief of staff for Attorney General Edwin Meese.

Levin is the former president of the Landmark Legal Foundation, a contributes commentary to various media outlets such as National Review Online and a best-selling author of seven books. He also has been editor-in-chief of the Conservative Review and is best known for his incendiary commentary. Furthermore, Levin is known for his harsh criticisms of Democrats and encouragement of primary challenges to congressional Republicans that he considers to be “RINO”.

Mark Levin Legal and Political

Levin worked for Texas Instruments after law school. Starting in 1981, he worked as a counselor to several members of President Ronald Reagan’s cabinet. He started at ACTION, the federal agency that oversaw VISTA and other volunteer organizations, before filling in as deputy assistant secretary for elementary. He finally turned into the associate director of presidential personnel and ultimately chief of staff to Attorney General Edwin Meese.

In 2001 the American Conservative Union granted Levin its Ronald Reagan Award for his work with Landmark Legal. Politico detailed in 2014 that Levin earns a salary of more than $300,000 per year as president of the non-profit Landmark Legal Foundation, whose givers incorporate the Sarah Scaife Foundation. In 2018, Levin ventured down as president of Landmark Legal yet stayed on its board of directors.

Mark Levin Radio Broadcasting

Levin started his broadcasting profession as a visitor on conservative talk radio programs. For a long time, he was a regular supporter of legal opinions to The Rush Limbaugh Show. He was additionally a supporter to The Sean Hannity Show and lastly got his very own radio slot on WABC, initially on Sundays starting in 2002, at that point in the timeslot following Hannity’s program in 2003. Cumulus Media Networks started syndicating The Mark Levin Show broadly in 2006.

He has also taken part in Freedom Concerts, an annual benefit concert to aid families of fallen soldiers, and utilizations his radio program to promote aid to military families. Levin is likewise associated with Move America Forward, a charity that sends care bundles to soldiers serving overseas. On February 11, 2016, he signed a ten-year contract augmentation with Westwood One, which will take his show through 2025, its 19th year. On November 16, 2018, Levin was enlisted to the National Radio Hall of Fame.
