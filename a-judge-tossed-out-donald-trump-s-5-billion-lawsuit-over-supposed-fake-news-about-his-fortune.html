From the moment that Donald Trump first rose to fame, two things have been very clear about him. First off, Trump loves attention, which is why he enjoyed making so many movie cameos. Secondly, Trump has always loved to talk about how rich he is and things like how much he spent on renovating Mar-a-Lago.

Since Trump loves being known as rich and famous, he has always been deeply upset when people downplay how rich he is. However, it is amazing to learn that when one writer claimed Trump was a lot less wealthy than he tells people, the former President sued them for $5 billion.

Donald Trump Sued An Author For $5 Billion For Downplaying His Wealth

In 2005, an author named Timothy L. O'Brien released a book titled "TrumpNation: The Art of Being The Donald". While Trump often seems to believe that all publicity is good publicity, it certainly seems clear he didn't feel that way about this book. After all, he sued O'Brien for $5 billion over his book.

From the moment that Trump rose to fame, there has been a lot of discussion about how rich Trump really is.

When O'Brien released his book with that estimate, he had to know that would outrage Trump. In fact, since O'Brien wrote about Trump's past behavior, he should have anticipated that he may need to defend himself against the business leader in court.

That said, it seems all but certain that nobody would have anticipated how much money Trump wound up suing the author for. In 2009, Trump sued O'Brien for defamation, and he asked for a staggering sum of $5 billion.

Unfortunately for Trump, a judge ultimately threw out his $5 billion lawsuit. That said, it is important to note that the court throwing out the lawsuit doesn't mean that they deemed O'Brien's reporting on Trump's fortune to be accurate.

Instead, the court revealed in a ruling that they couldn't find that O'Brien acted with malice while writing about Trump's fortune.

While Trump had to be upset to have his lawsuit thrown out, things got worse from there. The reason for that is a relic from Trump's failed lawsuit against O'Brien has since been consistently used against the former President by his detractors.

Even now that it has been close to two decades since O'Brien published his book, there is still a lot of debate over how rich Trump is. In many cases, that is because Trump's detractors routinely argue that Trump isn't as wealthy as he or publications like Forbes claim.

Of course, it is nothing new for people to claim that the leaders of opposing political parties are liars. That said, Trump's detractors use a statement that the former President made during the legal process over his lawsuit against O'Brien against him.

As a result of his lawsuit against O'Brien, Trump wound up taking part in a deposition. While O'Brien's team questioned Trump, the former President stated that he raises and lowers his claims about his net worth based on his own feelings. That quote has been routinely wielded to question any reports about Trump's net worth ever since.
