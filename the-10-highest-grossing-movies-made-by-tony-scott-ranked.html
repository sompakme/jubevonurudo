The 10 Highest-Grossing Movies Made By Tony Scott, Ranked

An influential artist, Tony Scott began his career as a director by making commercials during the 1970s. His older brother Ridley Scott was an inspiration to him. MGM soon hired Tony to direct The Hunger in 1982, which paved a pathway for his future in Hollywood. Scott went on to direct some of the most critically and commercially successful movies loved by audiences globally. Best known to be the master of directing action thrillers, Scott had worked with Hollywood A-listers throughout his career, most notably with Denzel Washington, with whom he worked in five movies. The director has earned several accolades for his contribution to cinema, including a Primetime Emmy and a BAFTA Award.

From thrillers to action-comedy, Scott has made a breakthrough in challenging genres to create cinematic masterpieces that are remembered even today.

Man On Fire (2004)

Man On Fire is one of the most popular Denzel-Scott collaborations, adapted from a novel of the same name. Tony Scott initially wanted to direct the movie in the 1980s. However, the studio was not confident to spend a $70 million budget on a new director. Starring Denzel Washington, Dakota Fanning, and Christopher Walken, the movie was a success and made $130.9 million.

Spy Game (2001)

 

Tony Scott released Spy Game's action thriller, which casts two legendary actors from different generations. The movie stars Brad Pitt and Robert Redford, with Redford playing the role of a retiring CIA Agent, as stated by Indie Wire. As the movie switches between periods, Scott has managed to create both worlds effortlessly. The movie was shot on a $115 million budget while earning $143 million.

The Taking Of Pelham 123 (2009)

An action thriller with Denzel Washington and John Travolta in the lead, The Taking Of Pelham 123 is the third adaptation of a novel with the same name. It was previously adapted into a theatrical and TV film in 1974 and 1998. A movie about a train dispatcher, it grossed $152 million around the world against its $100 million budget.

Days Of Thunder (1990)

Days Of Thunder, produced by Paramount Pictures, is an action drama and stars Tom Cruise as the lead. As Cole Trickle, a young driver, Tom Cruise enters the world of NASCAR racing, as noted by IMDB. Released in June 1990, Days Of Thunder also starred Robert Duvall and Nicole Kidman in supporting roles, and the movie collected $157 million at the box office.

Crimson Tide (1995)

Tony Scott initially worked with Washington for Crimson Tide. A movie based on war, where the political turmoil between countries is shaken as the submarine captain decides to attack the opposition. While the film received mixed reviews from the audiences, it was a box office success and earned $159 million.

Unstoppable (2010)

Known as one of the best action thrillers made by Tony, Unstoppable was also his last directed film. According to The Hollywood Reporter, this action-thriller is based on a real-life inside of a runaway freight train. Unstoppable stars Denzel as a veteran railroad engineer with Chris Pine as the young conductor. A $100 million budget movie, it grossed $167.8 million in theaters worldwide.

 

Déjà Vu (2006)

Another Denzel Washington-starrer, Déjà Vu, is Scott's only science fiction thriller and earned $181 million worldwide. The movie sees Washington’s character trying to save New Orleans based on his information about the future. It has a stunning supporting cast, including Val Kilmer, Paula Patton, and Adam Goldberg.

Enemy Of The State (2008)

A fast-paced thriller starring Will Smith and Gene Hackman, Enemy Of The State, revolves around shocking footage that showcases the death of the Congressman and how a lawyer is wrongly imprisoned for a crime he didn’t commit. An $80 million budget movie, it was well-received by audiences and grossed $250.6 million in theaters.

Beverly Hills Cop II (1987)

The only action-comedy on the list, Beverly Hills Cop II, is a sequel to the Beverly Hills Cop series. Eddie Murphy was the protagonist and played the role of police detective Axel Foley. The film was a box office success, making $276.5 million on a $27 million budget, and was even nominated for an Oscar and a Golden Globe for Best Original Song.

Top Gun (1986)

Tony’s most successful movie to date has been Top Gun. As mentioned by Screen Rant, Tom Cruise, a pop culture icon, played the lead in the film and remained one of the most famous characters of the 21st century. With iconic lines and cinematography, the action-adventure movie raked $357.4 million and became the highest-grossing movie of 1986.

Tony Scott’s other notable movies are In Her Shoes, The Grey, and The Last Boy Scout. He has influenced some of the most popular up-and-coming directors who have tried to bring magic to action-thrillers similar to Scott’s. While the director may be gone, his legacy still lives on through his movies. Top Gun will also be releasing a highly-anticipated sequel Top Gun: Maverick, in 2022.
