Elvis Presley Put On A "Country Boy Act" To Pick Up Women According To His Longtime Bodyguard

Since Elvis Presley is a true music legend, generations of fans have obsessed over every aspect of his life. In fact, fans are so curious about Presley that his former longtime bodyguard, Red West, became famous mostly due to his association with the rock star.

After Presley's father fired several members of the singer's entourage, including his bodyguard, West wrote a book about his former boss. That book, which was entitled "Elvis: What Happened", contained many fascinating facts about the rock star. Arguably the most interesting passage from the book is when West wrote about Presley adopting a persona to pick up women.

“He really developed that professional country-boy act with women. In the later years it was no longer natural, it became his line.”

As West revealed later in his book, the reason why Presley had to put on the persona around women as time went on is his personality changed. After several years of having women interested in him, Presley wasn't nervous around women anymore.

“It was pretty funny, but at the time I thought, heck, man, old Elvis is starting to lose his shyness. Elvis then started to relax a lot more when there were gals around.”
